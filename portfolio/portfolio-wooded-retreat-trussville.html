Wooded Retreat – Trussville

Tucked deep into a wooded hillside, this home was built to feel peaceful, grounded, and connected to the land. Every choice—from the materials to the layout—was made to create a comfortable family home that feels right at home in its surroundings.

Location Trussville, Alabama
Custom BuildBrick & Board-and-BattenWooded Lot

Project Overview

A grounded home built for real life among the trees.

This project sits on a private, heavily wooded lot in Trussville where the goal was simple: build a family home that feels both strong and peaceful. We used a mix of red brick and board-and-batten siding to bring warmth and texture, while keeping the design timeless and low-maintenance. The layout takes advantage of the slope, giving the home a walk-out lower level that opens toward the woods and connects indoor life with the landscape.

One of my favorite details on this build is how it balances practicality and beauty—the detached metal workshop, the deep front porch, and the way natural light moves through the interior. It’s a home built for everyday living, done the right way.

Home Highlights

  • Open-concept main level with large windows and forest views
  • Board-and-batten siding paired with red brick for timeless curb appeal
  • Walk-out basement designed for functional living and storage
  • Detached steel workshop with dual roll-up doors
  • Private wooded lot offering both privacy and connection to nature

Signature Details

  • Black-trimmed windows that frame the surrounding trees
  • Stained timber porch posts and metal roof accents
  • Durable, low-maintenance finishes throughout
  • Custom white cabinetry with matte black hardware
  • Warm oak flooring and soft, neutral interior palette

Material Story

Honest materials, built to last.

This home brings together a simple, durable palette—brick, board-and-batten siding, black windows, and timber details— all chosen to fit the wooded setting and stand strong for generations.

Brick & board-and-batten exterior

Red brick grounds the home with warmth and texture, while vertical board-and-batten siding adds contrast and character for a timeless Southern look.

Black windows & metal roof accents

Black-trimmed windows frame wooded views and bring in light, while metal roof details add strength and style.

Timber porch posts & custom entry

Stained wood beams and brackets craft a welcoming entry that balances structure with handcrafted detail.

Detached steel workshop

Durable steel framing, dual roll-up doors, and clean lines give the workshop practical utility that fits the site.

Interior palette of wood and light

White cabinetry, matte black hardware, and oak flooring create calm, livable interiors warmed by natural light.
