About Alabaster

Connected to Birmingham. Rooted in Shelby County charm.

Just twenty-five minutes south of downtown Birmingham, Alabaster, Alabama (35007) balances small-town warmth with metropolitan access. Winding through I-65 and Highway 31, the city is surrounded by wooded hillsides, parks, and well-established neighborhoods that invite thoughtful design and comfortable living. From family-friendly communities like Weatherly and Navajo Hills to emerging developments near Veterans Park, Alabaster offers diverse opportunities for custom home construction and refined renovation.

Riveroak Building Company partners with homeowners who value detail, transparency, and a home that reflects their lifestyle. Our design-build process integrates architecture, engineering, and craftsmanship—so each home feels individually composed while remaining harmonious with its environment. We plan each project around its site conditions, sun orientation, drainage patterns, and views, ensuring lasting beauty and structural integrity.

Alabaster’s growth brings new opportunities, but also requires experience with its Shelby County permitting systems, neighborhood covenants, and HOA review processes. Riveroak coordinates these details on your behalf, maintaining progress while protecting architectural intent. Whether your goal is a modern farmhouse on a wooded lot or a transitional estate near the city’s center, our process delivers balance—between timeless design and modern livability.

With every build, we remain true to our core promise: Honest. Fair. Reliable. From feasibility studies to final walk-throughs, we manage every phase with clarity and respect, ensuring your Alabaster home becomes part of the area’s legacy of craftsmanship and care.

FAQ

Alabaster home building questions—answered.

What impacts the price of a custom home in Alabaster?

The price of building a custom home in Alabaster, Alabama (35007) depends on a variety of factors—including lot topography, soil conditions, utility access, and the architectural style and finishes you select. Riveroak Building Company conducts an in-person site evaluation to identify grade, drainage, and connection points before delivering a comprehensive, transparent estimate. Our process ensures accurate pricing for excavation, framing, and design features while aligning every detail with your budget and vision.

Do you handle city permits and inspections for Alabaster homes?

Yes. Riveroak manages all City of Alabaster permits and Shelby County inspections, including coordination with neighborhood architectural review committees (ARCs) and homeowners’ associations. Whether your lot is in Ballantrae, Weatherly, or the Siluria Historic District, we handle every document submission, utility tie-in, and inspection appointment—streamlining approvals and minimizing delays so your Alabaster home build stays on schedule.

Can you help prepare the land before construction?

Absolutely. We provide complete site preparation and land development services prior to home construction in Alabaster. Our team evaluates grading, drainage, soil composition, and foundation readiness to ensure a stable building environment. We also assist with driveway placement, stormwater management, and retaining wall design where needed—partnering with licensed engineers and surveyors to make sure your site is fully optimized before the first footing is poured.

Do you also take on renovation or addition projects in Alabaster?

Yes—we specialize in whole-home renovations, luxury additions, and outdoor living spaces throughout Alabaster, AL. From kitchen and bathroom remodels to garage apartments, pool houses, and screened porches, we design seamless expansions that complement your home’s existing architecture. Riveroak brings the same attention to detail and communication to renovations as we do to new construction—delivering upgrades that enhance livability, beauty, and long-term value.

How long does it take to build a custom home in Alabaster?

Most custom homes in Alabaster take between 8 to 14 months from groundbreaking to move-in, depending on design complexity, permitting timelines, and weather conditions. We provide a detailed project schedule with milestone walkthroughs so you can follow each phase— from framing and mechanical installations to trim and final inspection. Clear communication and realistic timelines are central to Riveroak’s design-build process.
